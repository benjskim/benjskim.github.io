Overview

Upaji was a social dining web application connecting cooks with potential guests who were looking to experience an authentic dining experience. I was hired as their UX Design Intern in the summer of 2014 with the intended tasks of anything ranging from redesigning their home page to critiquing user flows.

This experience was not without its difficulties. Because I was the only tech person in the US team (while the rest of the tech team resided in India/Pakistan), progress was often impeded by communication hardships and vision differences with the Product Manager, a learning experience I will address in the "Other Challenges" section. Once news spread that the startup was eventually folding, all development halted and employees started to leave. As a result, my internship lasted only 9 weeks without much progress. Overall, working at Upaji was a fantastic learning experience; I have nothing but positive memories of my time there.

Roles and Goals

My time at Upaji was a little bumpy before I even started. I was originally supposed to work as the UX/Marketing combo intern due to my background in marketing. Because I had such little experience with UX, I wanted to contribute in marketing and use the experience of assisting the other UX intern as a transition to a UXD career. However, the other intern ultimately accepted another job offer and the sole responsibility of UX fell to my lap. Maybe it was a little ego and a little impatience, but with confidence I accepted the additional responsibility.
