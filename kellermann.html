Overview

(As you can probably tell, this project page is currently under construction. Check back soon for complete details!)
I'm really excited to share this project. During college I took several courses in social entrepreneurship, nonprofits, and sustainability that really changed the way I saw economic and social progress around the world. So when it came to building experience for my UX career, I wanted to spend time volunteering for awesome nonprofits who otherwise couldn't afford design talent.

Thanks to Catchafire.org, I was introduced to Ms. Sally Stillings, the Executive Director of the Kellermann Foundation. KF is doing phenomenal things in Uganda - their core focus being education, economic development, and health in the Batwa region. What I admire most is the foundation's approach to long-term sustainability, empowering the remote tribe of Ugandan Batwa pygmies instead of creating a level of dependency.

Like many nonprofits, their website hasn't been updated in many, many years. And with rising competition from other web-savvy nonprofits, a better UX is key for nonprofits like KF for a number of reasons, as I'll explain below.

Approaching the Project
Old version of the website.
(Click to enlarge)

To be quite honest, I didn't know how I would approach this project. Since it was my first probono gig, I wanted to be ambitious, build the entire site, and get experience in every step to the design/development process. However, I quickly realized I lacked the necessary skills (and time) to not rely on CMS platforms like WordPress. During our initial interview, I explained to Ms. Stillings that we'd most likely use WordPress and if her staff wasn't familiar with the system, I'd be happy to teach them the basics. We agreed that'd be our best shot at success.

I knew early on that this project will be more than just a re-design of their website, but rather an overhaul of their online revenue strategy. As you probably already know, nonprofits like KF rely on donations to maintain their projects abroad. However, most nonprofits haven't grasped the power of online fundraising just yet because they are often understaffed.
